Abstract

Advanced technologies have dramatically changed the ways that accounting professionals collect, analyze and interpret data. Embracing the prevalent implementation of advanced technologies, accounting is moving towards a STEM discipline and requesting accounting students be equipped with emerging technologies.

Key Points:

  • Response to increasing AACSB and CPA exam requirements on data analytics skills
  • Complete data analytics process using SQL and Tableau
  • Real-world data analysis with Dillard's department store data
  • Interactive dashboards and narrative stories for communication
  • Building data analytics mindset for accounting students

The Case Study

Dillard's Department Store Analysis

Dillard's, Inc. is a high-end department store chain based in Little Rock, Arkansas, operating approximately 282 stores across 29 states. The case involves analyzing the cosmetic department's performance using real transactional data.

Key Findings & Results

Geographic Performance

  • Texas leads with 57 stores, followed by Florida with 42
  • Most states met the 5% discount target in December 2015
  • Iowa showed significant discount rate increases post-Christmas
  • Arkansas maintained lowest discount rates but lower profit margins

Brand Performance

  • Top 5 brands: Clinique, Estée Lauder, Lancôme, MAC, Chanel
  • Consistent popularity across different states
  • Regional preferences identified between Florida and Louisiana
  • Brand clustering revealed four distinct performance groups

Weekly Patterns

  • Thursday and Friday showed higher discount rates
  • Saturday sales were highest despite lower discounts
  • No clear correlation between discount rates and sales volume
  • Iowa consistently offered highest discount rates

Growth Trends

  • Significant sales fluctuations in 2015 (Jan-Aug)
  • Smoother decline patterns in 2016
  • Sharp drops in March and August 2015
  • Predicted 20% growth rate for February 2017

Return Analysis

  • Dillard's outperformed industry average (5.21%)
  • Higher return rates in January (post-Christmas)
  • Stable 3-4% rates between February-October
  • Gift card transactions had highest return rates

Business Recommendations

  • Focus on Saturday promotional activities
  • Reduce Thursday discount rates to improve margins
  • Implement targeted strategies for different brand clusters
  • Consider alternative promotional methods beyond discounts
